1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & MODEL APPROACH
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The core challenge in this dataset is covariate shift and scale discrepancy. The training data on Day 49 only contains early morning records (00:00 to 02:00), whereas the test set contains Day 49 daytime records (02:15 to 13:45). Direct prediction models fail because they cannot extrapolate absolute demand.

To solve this, we formulated a Log-Ratio Ensemble Model:
* Instead of predicting raw demand, the model predicts the relative multiplier (log-ratio) of demand on Day 49 compared to the spatial-temporal baseline of Day 48.
* Target Formulation:
  y_diff = log(demand + 0.01) - log(geo_time_te + 0.01)
  where 'geo_time_te' is the historical demand baseline computed strictly on Day 48 data.
* Ensemble: We trained a blended ensemble of LightGBM, XGBoost, and CatBoost.
* Generalization: Since the day-level scale multiplier is relatively flat, the tree models learn a constant shift for Day 49 and generalize it seamlessly to daytime hours.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. FEATURE ENGINEERING DETAILS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Base32 Geohash Decoding: Decoded string geohashes into raw Latitude and Longitude coordinate floats, allowing models to learn spatial proximity features.
* Hierarchical Slicing: Created geohash prefixes (geohash_3, geohash_4, geohash_5) to capture spatial hierarchies.
* Time Dynamics: Extracted 'minutes', 'hour', and cyclic time variables using time_sin and time_cos (np.sin/cos of time of day in minutes / 1440.0) to model peak traffic periods.
* Strict Day 48 Baseline Target Encoding (TE):
  - Created target encodings for 'geohash', 'timestamp', and 'geo_time' (geohash + timestamp combination).
  - Out-of-fold statistics and test mappings are calculated EXCLUSIVELY on Day 48 data. This guarantees zero target leakage (no lookahead bias).
