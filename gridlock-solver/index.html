Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning
Uses Q-learning (e.g., DQN) or policy-based (PPO) agents at each junction.

State space: Queue lengths, waiting times, and incoming vehicle distances. Action space: Selection of traffic light phases. Reward function: Negative sum of waiting times of all vehicles.

Pros: Excellent at adapting to highly complex, non-linear flow dynamics. Cons: High computation requirements, requires SUMO simulation for training, and lacks guaranteed convergence limits (stability).
Spatio-Temporal Graph Networks
Utilizes models like STGCN (Spatio-Temporal Graph Convolutional Networks) to predict traffic speeds and flow volumes.

Spatial Convolutions: Learn correlations between adjacent roads based on the graph adjacency matrix. Temporal Convolutions: Capture time-series trends (rush hours, rain patterns).

Allows the system to *proactively* adjust timings or route vehicles via navigation apps before actual gridlock develops.
Floating Car Data (FCD) Fusion
Fuses stationary CCTV camera feeds with real-time GPS coordinates of delivery fleets (e.g., Flipkart trucks/bikes) and ride-hailing services.

Mechanism: Kalman filtering or Bayesian filters fuse the density measured by cameras at nodes with velocity vectors measured by vehicles along edges.

Solves the problem of "blind spots" where CCTV coverage is not available.
